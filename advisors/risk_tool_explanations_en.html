With the help of an interactive program, the individuals were able to visualize the risks associated with different investment options before making their investment decision.

The individuals were tasked with imagining that they wanted to invest {{ C.budget_string }} dollars over a period of {{ C.Anlagehorizont }} years. They could invest the money in two different types of investments:

  • A traditional savings account - a secure investment option with a low but fixed interest rate.
  • An investment in an ETF (exchange-traded fund) on the stock market – this allows you to benefit from potential capital gains and dividends, but also exposes you to the risk that the value of your investment may fluctuate or decrease.
You could decide how to divide your money between these two options – from putting everything into one of the two options to any combination in between. To give you a better idea of how different allocations affect your final payment, we have developed a simulated investment tool.. This tool showed you the results that were achieved in the past with similar investments.

Every 50th participant in this study received the actual value of the simulated portfolio after an investment period of {{ C.Anlagehorizont }} years, paid out in cents.

Example: If the simulated final payment of a person was {{ C.budget_string }} dollars, they received {{ Auszahlung }} dollars as a payout.

  • The simulation was based on real historical market data.
  • The payout was made within one month after participation.

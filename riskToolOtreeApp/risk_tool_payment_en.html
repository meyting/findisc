We would now like to illustrate the risks associated with different investment options using an interactive program, before you make your investment decision.

Imagine that you would like to invest ${{ C.budget_string }} over a period of {{ C.Anlagehorizont }} years. You can choose between two types of investments:

  • A traditional savings account – a safe option with low but fixed interest rates.
  • An investment in an ETF (exchange-traded fund) on the stock market – this allows you to benefit from potential price increases and dividends, but also involves the risk that the value of your investment may fluctuate or decrease.
You can decide how to split your money between these two options – from putting everything into one option to any combination in between. To give you a better idea of how different allocations could affect your final wealth, we have developed a simulated investment tool. It shows you what results similar investments have achieved in the past.

Important:

Every 50th participant in this study will receive a payout equal to the actual final value of the simulated portfolio, paid in cents.

Example: If your simulated final wealth is {{ C.budget_string }} euros, you will receive a payout of {{ Auszahlung }} euros.

  • The simulation is based on real historical market data.
  • The payout will be made within one month after your participation.

Please click "Next" to continue and specify your preferred allocation between the two investment options.
